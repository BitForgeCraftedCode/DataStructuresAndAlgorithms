# Recap: Bubble Sort & Selection Sort

Bubble Sort and Selection Sort are purely educational algorithms.\
They are introduced to explain how sorting works, not because they are
used in real-world systems.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Common Characteristics

-   **Time complexity:** O(n²)\
-   **Extra memory:** O(1) (in-place)\
-   **Scalability:** Poor\
-   **Practical usage:** None

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Key Difference

-   **Bubble Sort** repeatedly swaps adjacent elements.\
-   **Selection Sort** selects the maximum (or minimum) element on each
    pass and places it into its final position.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Stability

-   **Bubble Sort:** Stable\
-   **Selection Sort:** Unstable

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Final Takeaway

These algorithms are not important because of their performance,\
but because they are the first clear examples of algorithmic
inefficiency\
and help build intuition about time complexity.

In practice, encountering Bubble Sort or Selection Sort in production
code\
is usually a red flag, not a feature.
